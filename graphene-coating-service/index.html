Is Graphene Coating Worth the Upgrade?

Graphene is engineered to be harder and slicker than standard ceramic, with better heat dispersion that reduces water-spotting on hot panels — a real advantage in Indian summers. It also tends to last longer, so the cost per year of protection often works out very well.

As with any coating, the result depends on preparation. Every Carmaa graphene job begins with a full wash, clay decontamination and correction so the coating bonds to clean, smooth paint for maximum gloss and durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Longer-duration plans use more layers and higher-grade graphene with more thorough prep, giving stronger gloss, water sheeting and durability. 1-Year is ₹7499, 3-Year is ₹14999, and 5-Year is ₹24999.

Graphene is generally harder, slicker and better at dispersing heat (which reduces water spots), and it often lasts longer than ceramic. Ceramic is still excellent and more affordable — graphene is the premium upgrade.

Depending on the plan and care, graphene coating lasts roughly 1 to 5 years. Gentle washing and avoiding harsh chemicals helps it reach its full rated life.

It strongly resists swirl marks and minor marring and makes washing safer, but no coating is fully scratch-proof. For stone chips and impact, paint protection film (PPF) is the stronger choice.

Wait about 7 days for the coating to fully cure, then wash gently with a pH-neutral shampoo. Your detailer will provide complete aftercare guidance.
