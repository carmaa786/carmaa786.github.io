How to Take Care of Your Car During These Heavy Summers

Let's be honest—when the summer heat hits hard, we’re all rushing to crank up the AC or find some shade. But while you’re keeping cool, have you thought about what that intense, blazing sun is doing to your car? Prolonged exposure to heavy summer heat isn't just uncomfortable; it's practically a torture test for your vehicle’s engine, tires, and paint. Let’s talk about how to protect your ride and keep it running smoothly when the temperatures start soaring.

1. The Silent Threat: Coolant and Fluids

Think of coolant as your engine’s sweat. When it's working hard in the heat, it relies on that fluid to keep from having a total meltdown. But extreme heat makes engine fluids evaporate and burn off much faster than usual. Make it a habit to pop the hood and check your coolant levels (when the engine is cold, of course!). While you're at it, double-check your motor oil, brake fluid, and windshield wiper fluid. Keeping these topped off is the easiest way to avoid being stranded on the side of a melting highway with smoke billowing from your hood.

2. Tires: The Pressure Cooker Effect

Have you ever noticed more tire blowouts during the summer? There's science behind it. As the temperature rises, the air inside your tires expands. If your tires are already overinflated, driving on scorching hot asphalt increases the pressure even further, leading to a nasty blowout. On the flip side, underinflated tires generate more friction, which also causes excessive heat. Grab a tire pressure gauge and check your tires every week. Keeping them at the manufacturer's recommended level is a massive lifesaver.

3. Your Paint Job Is Fading

You wouldn't spend a day at the beach without sunscreen, right? Your car’s paint feels the same way. The UV rays from heavy summer sun break down the clear coat, leaving the paint looking dull and oxidized. To fight this, try to park in the shade whenever possible. Even better, invest in a good quality UV-resistant wax or a professional ceramic coating. It acts like SPF 50 for your car, reflecting those harmful rays and keeping that glossy shine intact.

4. The Oven Inside: Cabin Protection

We’ve all experienced the sheer agony of touching a burning steering wheel or hot leather seats. When parked in direct sunlight, the inside of your car can easily reach 60°C. This bakes your dashboard, causing plastics to crack and leather to dry out. A simple, affordable windshield sunshade goes a long way. Additionally, make sure to get your AC vents professionally sanitized. Summer is prime time for mold to grow in the AC condenser, leading to weird smells and poor cooling just when you need it most.
