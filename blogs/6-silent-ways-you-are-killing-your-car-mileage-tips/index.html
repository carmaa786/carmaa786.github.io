6 Silent Ways You Are Killing Your Car (And How to Boost Your Mileage)

Owning a car is a dream for many, but maintaining it often feels like a chore. Most car owners in India unknowingly develop habits that don't just reduce the car's lifespan but also burn a hole in their pockets through poor fuel efficiency and high repair costs. At Carmaa, we see these issues every day. Here are the 6 silent killers of your vehicle and how you can flip the script to get better mileage.

1. Ignoring the "Check Engine" Light

It starts as a small orange icon and ends with a broken-down vehicle. Many drivers ignore it if the car "feels fine." However, that light often signals a faulty oxygen sensor or a loose gas cap—both of which can tank your fuel economy by up to 40%.

2. Low Tire Pressure (The Silent Fuel Thief)

Driving on under-inflated tires increases rolling resistance. Your engine has to work harder, consuming more fuel. Checking your tire pressure weekly can boost your mileage by 3-5% instantly.

3. Carrying Unnecessary Weight

Is your trunk full of junk? Every extra 45kg reduces your fuel economy by about 1-2%. Clear out the clutter to let your engine breathe easier and save on fuel.

4. Aggressive Driving

Rapid acceleration and hard braking waste fuel and kill your brake pads. Smooth, gradual acceleration keeps the engine in its optimal RPM range, significantly improving highway mileage.

5. Skipping Air Filter Changes

A clogged air filter starves your engine of oxygen. It’s like trying to run while wearing a mask. Replacing a dirty air filter can improve the performance of older cars significantly.

6. Using the Wrong Oil

Always stick to the manufacturer’s recommended grade. Using thicker oil than required creates internal friction, forcing the engine to work harder and consume more fuel.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How often should I check my tire pressure?

For the best mileage, check your tire pressure every week or before any long trip. Even a 2 PSI drop can affect fuel efficiency.

2. Does a clean car really get better mileage?

Yes! A clean car has less aerodynamic drag. Professional detailing also protects your sensors and parts from grime that can cause mechanical resistance.
